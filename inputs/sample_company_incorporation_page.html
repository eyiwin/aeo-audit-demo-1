How do I incorporate a company in Singapore?

To incorporate a company in Singapore, you need to choose a company name, prepare KYC documents, appoint at least one local resident director, appoint a corporate secretary, provide a registered office address, and file the company registration with ACRA.

Can a foreigner start a company in Singapore?

Yes. A foreign shareholder can own a Singapore private limited company, but the company must have at least one local resident director. Harbour Incorporation can explain nominee director options and compliance requirements.

What documents are needed for Singapore company incorporation?

Typical documents include passport copies, proof of address, proposed company name, shareholder details, director details, company constitution, and KYC documents. Requirements may vary based on ownership structure and ACRA checks.

Evidence and source support

Singapore company registration is handled through ACRA, the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority. For example, companies must maintain a registered office address and appoint a corporate secretary after incorporation.

FAQ

How long does Singapore company registration take?

Most straightforward Singapore company registrations can be prepared quickly once KYC documents are complete, although approval timing depends on ACRA review and name approval.

What should I do after my Singapore company is incorporated?

After incorporation, you should set up statutory records, appoint or confirm your corporate secretary, open a business bank account, track IRAS tax obligations, and prepare for annual return filing.
