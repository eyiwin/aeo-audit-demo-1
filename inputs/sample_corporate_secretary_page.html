What does a corporate secretary do after incorporation?

A corporate secretary maintains statutory registers, prepares resolutions, supports annual return filing, tracks company changes, and helps directors understand ACRA compliance requirements.

Why it matters for new companies

The corporate secretary role helps a private limited company stay consistent with Singapore compliance expectations. This supports directors, shareholders, registered office address records, and IRAS-related planning.
